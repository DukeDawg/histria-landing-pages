Why These Books

The canon you quote without knowing it

“It”

The word that named celebrity

Elinor Glyn coined “It” in her 1927 novel — and a century of celebrity culture followed. Paramount paid her $50,000 for the concept alone, and the film made Clara Bow the biggest star of the Jazz Age.

✦

Nobel & Pulitzer pedigree

Sinclair Lewis — first American to win the Nobel Prize in Literature. Booth Tarkington — two-time Pulitzer winner. These authors defined American letters, then went quietly out of print.

†

The book behind Dracula

Bram Stoker read Emily Gerard's account of Transylvania — and then wrote Dracula. Her novel, The Waters of Hercules, is on the rescue list.
